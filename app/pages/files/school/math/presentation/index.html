A Brief History of Fractions


(Use down arrow)

Earliest Fractions

The earliest fractions were simple, they could show one half, one third, one forth, and so on using symbols.

Egyptian Fractions

Egyptian fractions were the sum of multiple unit fractions, ex:
1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8

Greek Fractions

Greek fractions were unit fractions as well as continued fractions later on.

Modern Fractions

Modern fractions first appeared around the year 500.

Adding Fractions


(Use down arrow)

Step One

Find a common denominator.
1/6 + 1/12 = ?
2/12 + 1/12 = ?

Step Two

Add the numerators.
2/12 + 1/12 = ?
2/12 + 1/12 = 3/12

Step Three

Simplify
3/12 = 1/4
Done!

Subtracting Fractions


(Use down arrow)

Step One

Find a common denominator.
1/6 - 1/12 = ?
2/12 - 1/12 = ?

Step Two

Subtract the numerators.
2/12 - 1/12 = ?
2/12 - 1/12 = 1/12

Step Three

Simplify.
1/12 = 1/12
Done!

Multiplying Fractions


(Use down arrow)

Step One

Multiply the 2 denominators and numerators.
2/3 * 1/2 = 2/6

Step Two

Simplify.
2/6 = 1/3
Done!
